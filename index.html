Compilers & programming

Compilers

Way before compilers were made computing and programming was a whole different deal

Machine Code

Is the base and the only language that a computer can speak, is made of raw binary instructions! Can you believe that? Just 0's and 1's.

Pseudo-code

A way to write instructions in a more comprehensive way and then it can be translated into binary machine language. Is awesome!.

Assembler

Eventually programmers made a mapper from that pseudo-code to binary, then assembler came into the picture. And no, it's not a compiler.

1st Compiler

In 1952 Dr Grace Hopper made the first compiler, taking a high level programming language and translate it into machine code, one high level instruction would take several binary instructions, way easier don't you think?

Formula translation

In 1957 IBM invented it's own compiler which dominated early computing programming but only worked in a single type of computer, and only into IBM computers, convenient right?.

1st Programming Language

In 1959 COBOL was created and the "write once, run anywhere" notion was born, that can run in any computer using the COBOL compiler. It kept scalating until where we are now, with so many programming languages for you to choose your fighter and do your part, heck yeah!

Computer Evolution

No matter how good software came to be, if not for hardware evolution and improvement none of the awesome things we're used to would even exist

  • 2500 B.C - 1890

    Manual or mechanical "computers"

    From the abacus to the Charles Babbage's difference engine and analytical engine this were devices wether manual or mechanic that did one thing, calculate of course, could been the time, the range of a cannon or even just add two numbers together!. Then punch cards were born.
    Fun fact: A calculator until the late 1800's was a person who did calculations and wroted them into books of tabulated values so that other people can save time and effort.

  • 1890 - 1946

    Electromechanical computers

    Electro mechanical computers

    In this particular set of devices the brain was made of mechanical relays but as any mechanical piece it usualy tends to wear and tear, so in the need for a better device the vacuum tube was born! This new pieces were used in the Colossus Mark I used to break nazi codes during WWII! But still a better technology was needed to acomplish the needs of men
    Fun fact: As the relays were warm insects used to get inside them, that's where the term bug in the computer appeared

  • 1946 - 1980

    Electric & Boolean computers

    In 1946 ENIAC was the 1st general purpose, programmable, electronic computer!. Thanks to Mr. George Boole and his own algebraic system, and the birth of the transistor we can now store data and instructions in a memory in our computer! This was the RAM's birth and the registers but still we need something to direct or execute this instructions or read the data. Right there is where a clock, a control unit and a ALU comes in, and poof there's a CPU. This particular structure of an ALU, a Control Unit, the registers and the RAM.

  • 1980 - Present

    John Vonn Neumann Era!

    Since the 50's stored-program computers included data and instructions in the same memory, but still in order to upload the program it was needed to use punch cards. That was a cheap but not efficient method to input your instructions inside a computer. Luckily in 1980 a new way to do that appeared, panel programming with switchs and lights, imput the program with just the switches, awesome! Eg: The Altair 8801. But that was way much complicated and not taking full advantage of what a computer can do rly. And then the evolution of the storage came!
